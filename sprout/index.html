What is Sprout?

In collaboration with the Greater Good Science Center and the Tang Center, Sprout is aimed at promoting the knowledge and practice of wellness-oriented behaviors among the UC Berkeley student body and staff.



What it'll look like

Users plant a tree that dynamically symbolizes their wellness growth. This branch will have branches which represent the fundamental tenets of wellness: gratitude, kindness, respect, empathy, purpose, awe, etc. Ssers will be able to select activities that relate to these “branches”, perform these activities, and grow their tree. Some activities include meditation or showing gratitude; we plan to be working with the “Greater Good in Action” applet: https://ggia.berkeley.edu/.

Features

  • Customize Your Journey

    Our questionaire will identify your wellness strengths and recommend activities to focus on

  • Track Your Progress

    Visualize your wellness journey with the growth of your tree branches whenever you complete an activity

  • Connect With Others

    Send letters of gratitude, attend wellness-oriented events on-campus, and encourage others on their progress

Documentation

Overview

The current version featured on this website is still under construction, and is set to change in the next couple of weeks. To start, users are recommended to log in using a gmail account so that their progress and category scores can be saved when they complete activities.



Home

Currently, the home tab display includes 12 separate score blocks that are personalized based on user. Each of the blocks in the display represents the user's score or progress for that specific tenet of wellness. A darker shade of green implies a greater score for that category. Users can hover over each block to see the category that it corresponds to.



Activities

The activity list displays the entire list of activities that Sprout recommends to its users. The bar above enables users to filter activities by category. When users complete a task, they can click on the respective button to add to their score for the categories of wellness which that activity corresponds to. The "Explore Activity" button will link to the Greater Good in Action page, from which users can find more in-depth explainations of the activity details and how it benefits one's personal wellbeing. Users are also able to share the activity with others.



Friends

The social aspect of the Sprout web application involves adding friends and viewing their name, location and personalized score block.
